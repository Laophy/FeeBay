# FeeBay Simulator — Steam Early Access Answers

## Why Early Access?

We built FeeBay Simulator as a deep economy game, and an economy is only as good as the players stress-testing it. Early Access lets us put the buying, grading, and flipping loop in players' hands so we can tune pricing, difficulty, and pacing against real playstyles instead of guesses. We'd rather grow the game alongside the community than ship it in a vacuum.

## Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

We plan for FeeBay Simulator to be in Early Access for roughly 6 to 12 months. This may shift depending on the scope of community feedback and the features we decide to pursue.

## How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

We plan to expand the full version with more cards and sets, additional marketplaces and market events, deeper progression and upgrade paths, and more ways to grow your reselling business. We also plan to refine economy balance, add quality-of-life improvements, and incorporate ideas that emerge from player feedback during Early Access. The current scope is a strong foundation, and we'd like to build outward from it rather than make fixed promises about exact features.

## What is the current state of the Early Access version?

The core game is fully playable. You can buy and flip cards across multiple marketplaces, send pulls to three grading companies, crack mystery lots and storage units, bid in auctions, run your own storefront, collect a full set of cards, purchase upgrades, chase achievements, and ride a live market of trends and events. Early Access is focused on balancing this experience and expanding its content, not on filling in missing core systems.

## Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

We plan to gradually raise the price as we add new content and features over the course of Early Access. Players who join early will get the game at its lowest price.

## How are you planning on involving the community in your development process?

We plan to use the Steam Community forums and discussions to gather feedback, take suggestions for new cards, sets, and features, and run polls on balance and direction. Player input on the economy and difficulty will directly shape how we tune and expand the game, and we plan to share regular updates on what we're working on.
